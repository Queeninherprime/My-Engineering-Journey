# Domain 1 — Security Principles


---

## 1. The CIA Triad
| Principle | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Confidentiality** | Only authorized users access data | Encryption, passwords |
| **Integrity** | Data is accurate and unaltered | Hashing, checksums |
| **Availability** | Systems accessible when needed | Backups, redundancy |

---

## 2. Authentication & Non-Repudiation
- **Authentication** — Proving who you are (password, MFA)
- **Authorization** — What you are allowed to do
- **Non-repudiation** — You cannot deny an action you took
- **Accountability** — Actions traced back to individuals

---

## 3. Risk Management
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Threat** | Potential cause of harm |
| **Vulnerability** | Weakness that can be exploited |
| **Risk** | Threat × Vulnerability × Impact |
| **Control** | Measure to reduce risk |

**Risk Responses:**
- **Accept** — Live with the risk
- **Avoid** — Stop the activity causing risk
- **Transfer** — Insurance or third party
- **Mitigate** — Reduce the risk

---

## 4. Security Governance
- **Policies** — High level rules (what to do)
- **Standards** — Specific requirements (how much)
- **Procedures** — Step by step instructions (how)
- **Guidelines** — Recommended best practices

---

## 5. Privacy vs Security
- **Security** — Protecting data from unauthorized access
- **Privacy** — Right of individuals to control their data
- **PII** — Personally Identifiable Information
